Introduction

The Courland Census 1797 Parish Census documents included Jewish House Lists.  Many of these Jews were innkeepers and wine distillers and lived at the inns at the various parish estates and small villages.   Included on these web pages are house lists for villages and estates in the political parishes of Dunaburg, Nerft, Ascheraden, Selburg, and Uberlautz.  These parishes were in the vicinity of the city of Jakobstadt, so most of the Jews in these House Lists registered in the Jakobstadt City Tax Registers in 1798.  For this reason, many of these individuals are included in the Jakobstadt Tax Registers and House Lists for 1803 through 1858 that appear on these web page. 
 
Unlike the other census documents on these web pages, the 1797 census documents are not presented in table format.  Instead, these Jewish House Lists are translated and presented as they appeared in the original documents.  For a more detailed explaination about the 1797 Courland Census click here.

Name Searches

In 1797, Jews did not have hereditary last names that were passed down from father to son.  Instead, many Jews used the father's name as a patronymic.  For example, Abraham Hirschowitz was Abraham, son of Hirsch.   It is important to understand when reviewing these pages that a patronymic in 1797 was probably not a family name that was passed down to later generations.  (Herediatry last names started to be used in Courland a few decades later.)
 
In order to facilitate searching through these documents, two indices were created:
 
Index by Surname or Patronymic for Future Records
There were a few dozen families on these 1797 House Lists that could be connected to later census records from 1803 through 1858.  This index shows those families.  They are identified by:
--future surname, which might be patronymic
--1797 patronymic (or first name of the father, if no patronymic)
--1797 first and middle name
--estate
--inn or property on the estate
--parish book number
--parish name
 
If you go to the referenced 1797 house list and click on a link by the name of the head of household, you will see a web page that lists future records for this family.
 
Index by Patronymic on the 1797 Census
This index includes individuals with patronymics who appear on these censuses.  The index is sorted by patronymic.  Listed are records for:
--Heads of Households
--Brothers of Heads of Households
--Father-in-Laws
--Son-in-Laws
--Brother-in-Laws
--Servants and Apprentices
--Wards
 
Please note that this index includes only those individuals with a patronymic.
